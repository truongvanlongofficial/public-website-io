Earthquake Radar Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 3, 2026

What the app does

Earthquake Radar helps users view recent earthquake activity on a map, filter events by timeframe and magnitude, and optionally receive local notifications for matching earthquake events.

Data and permissions

The app uses internet access to load earthquake data from public USGS feeds and map tiles. The app requests notification permission on Android 13 and newer only when needed to show earthquake monitor notifications.

The initial version does not request device location, camera, microphone, contacts, media, or account access.

Local storage

The app stores alert monitor settings locally on the device, including whether monitoring is enabled, the selected timeframe, the minimum magnitude threshold, and the last notified event identifier. These settings are used to keep notifications relevant.

Third-party services

The app connects to USGS earthquake feeds and map tile services to display current earthquake information and map content. These services may receive technical information such as IP address, request time, and device/network details as part of normal internet communication.

Ads, analytics, and purchases

The initial dashboard workflow does not require a subscription. The app contains advertising configuration for future monetization, but no ad placement is required to view the implemented map, feed, filters, or monitor settings. If third-party ads are enabled in a future release, ad providers may process device, ad, diagnostics, or usage data according to their own policies.

The app does not require an account, subscription, in-app purchase, or login for the implemented features.

Notifications

If monitoring is enabled and notification permission is granted, the app may periodically check the selected earthquake feed in the background and show a local notification when a new matching event is found. Android may delay background checks based on battery, network, or system settings.

Emergency information

Earthquake Radar is an information and monitoring app. Notifications are based on periodic feed checks after earthquake data is published by the data source. The app is not an emergency service and does not provide early warnings before shaking starts.

Contact

For privacy questions, contact the developer through the Google Play listing for this app.
